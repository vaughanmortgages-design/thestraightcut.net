FAQ

Common mortgage
questions answered.

What is the difference between a mortgage broker and a mortgage agent?

A mortgage agent (Level 1 or Level 2) is licensed under FSRA and works within a brokerage. A Level 2 agent can supervise other agents. Both can access multiple lenders on your behalf — unlike a bank that only offers their own products.

How much do your mortgage services cost?

In most cases, our services are completely free to you. We are compensated by the lender when your mortgage funds. Fees are only applicable in certain specialized situations, and we'll always be transparent about this upfront.

How do I qualify for a mortgage in Canada?

Qualification depends on your income, credit score, down payment, and debt levels. We'll review your complete financial picture and find lenders whose programs you qualify for — including alternative lenders if needed.

Can I get a mortgage if I'm self-employed?

Absolutely. We work with lenders who have programs specifically for self-employed Canadians, including stated income options and business-for-self programs that use bank statements rather than traditional T4 income.

What areas do you serve?

We primarily serve Vaughan, the City of Vaughan, Woodbridge, Maple, Kleinburg, Concord, Richmond Hill, Thornhill, Markham, King City, and the broader Greater Toronto Area.

When should I start the mortgage process?

As early as possible — ideally 3 to 6 months before you plan to buy. For renewals, start shopping 120 days before your renewal date. Early preparation gives you the best negotiating position and time to resolve any credit issues.
