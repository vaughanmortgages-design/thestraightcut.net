Amazon vs eBay Canada: where should you buy?

Both marketplaces can save you money, but they are good at different things. Amazon is usually stronger for speed, consistency and everyday essentials. eBay is often better for refurbished electronics, niche parts, collectibles and hard-to-find items.

Choose Amazon when…

  • You want fast shipping and simple returns.
  • You are buying common household, kitchen, electronics or gift items.
  • You prefer new products and predictable checkout.
  • You want to compare best sellers, new releases and deal pages quickly.

Choose eBay when…

  • You are hunting for refurbished electronics or open-box gear.
  • You need auto parts, replacement parts or discontinued items.
  • You want to compare multiple sellers and condition levels.
  • You are shopping for collectibles or harder-to-find products.

Price is not the only thing to compare

Look at the total cost after shipping, taxes and any import charges. On eBay, check seller ratings, item condition and return terms. On Amazon, compare the seller name and whether the item is fulfilled by Amazon or shipped by a third party.

Best place for refurbished electronics

eBay usually gives you more selection across used, open-box and refurbished products. Amazon can still be useful, but condition descriptions and seller quality vary. For expensive electronics, compare warranty coverage and return windows before buying.

Best place for everyday shopping

Amazon usually wins for convenience. The checkout is faster, shipping is more predictable, and categories such as kitchen, home, pet supplies and small electronics are easy to browse.

Best overall strategy

Use Amazon first for standard retail items and fast delivery. Use eBay when you are searching for a better price on refurbished gear, unusual products or replacement parts. Checking both before a larger purchase is often worth the extra minute.
